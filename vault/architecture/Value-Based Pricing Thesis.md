# Value-Based Pricing Thesis

The core narrative. Danni doesn't just use x402 - she embodies why x402 exists.

## The Death of Seat-Based SaaS

**Old model:** Price access. $99/seat/month. Value disconnected from usage. A tax on potential.

**New model:** Price outcomes. $100 per strategic brief. Value = deliverable. Intent → payment → result. One HTTP cycle.

## Intent → Value Pricing

As agentic commerce matures, pricing structures shift fundamentally:

| Dimension | Old World (SaaS/Consulting) | New World (Agentic Commerce) |
|---|---|---|
| **Unit of pricing** | Seat/month, hourly rate | Per outcome, per intent |
| **Relationship** | Subscription, retainer | Transactional, on-demand |
| **Speed** | Costs more (rush fees) | Is the default (60 seconds) |
| **Overhead** | Meetings, onboarding, kickoffs | Zero. The request IS the engagement |
| **Price signal** | Time spent, team size | Value delivered |
| **Commitment** | Monthly/annual lock-in | Per-request. No commitment beyond the ask |

## Why x402 Enables This

The HTTP request IS the intent. The 402 response IS the price. The payment IS the commitment. The response IS the value.

The entire commercial relationship compressed into a single HTTP exchange. No accounts, no subscriptions, no sales calls. Just value for money, settled on-chain.

## Danni as Embodiment

Danni is not "cheaper McKinsey." She's proof that McKinsey's model is obsolete for many use cases.

- You don't hire Danni
- You don't subscribe to Danni
- You don't book a meeting with Danni
- You ask Danni a question and pay for the answer
- 60 seconds later, you have a strategic brief backed by 5 parallel analysts and real market data
- The relationship lasts exactly as long as the value exchange

$100 for a brief that would cost $15,000 and two weeks from a consultancy. Nobody blinks at the price because it reflects the **value**, not the **process**.

## The Speed Relationship

Old: Velocity is limited by human coordination. Speed costs extra.
New: Velocity is the default. AI agents operate at the speed of inference. The constraint shifts from "how fast" to "how valuable."

Man hours → irrelevant
Meetings → irrelevant
Retainers → irrelevant
The deliverable → the only thing that matters
