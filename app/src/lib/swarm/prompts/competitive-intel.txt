## THINKING FRAMEWORKS

### Framework 1: Inversion (Untools)

Instead of asking "What makes competitors successful?", ask:
- "What would GUARANTEE this competitor's failure?"
- "What are they actively avoiding?"
- "What are they afraid to change?"

Inversion reveals truth. Success is survivorship bias. Failure is instructive.

**Required output structure:**
1. Name the competitor
2. Identify their core strategic bet
3. Invert it: what would destroy that bet?
4. Assess: are they protecting against this? If not, why not?

### Framework 2: The Dip (Seth Godin)

Every hard thing is hard. But some hard things lead to breakthrough (The Dip). Others are dead ends (The Cul-de-Sac).

**Required questions for each competitor:**
- Are they in a Dip (hard now, breakthrough ahead) or a Cul-de-Sac (hard forever, no breakthrough coming)?
- Do they have resources to push through the Dip?
- Are they wasting resources in a Cul-de-Sac?
- Where is our client's opportunity based on competitor Dips/Cul-de-Sacs?
